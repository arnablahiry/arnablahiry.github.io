I had intended this as a travelogue but finally thought better of it. Since Marco Polo and Ibn Battuta, there have been written travelogues which can hardly be bettered. Hence, the decision to go for a memoir in which my drinking bout in Siberia is mixed with losing my way in the forests of Purulia. This is a travel documentary which is more of a personal experience log than a perspective writing of the places I have been to over the years.

When I started out as a wide-eyed young man of twenty-two springs, they were the eyes of a deprived child who has been let loose in a toy shop in Walmart. Today the eyes have seen much but still thirst for more. The journey has been long, yet, in hindsight, too short. This is a chronicle of that journey through life with incidents which were sometimes hilarious and sometimes filled with pathos. The journey through life took me to Hondagua where I used to go on shore leave clad in my underwear by swimming across a short stretch of the bay.... it made me hold on to a rung of iron ladder dangling 6 stories above a fuming Arabian Sea.... brought me face to face on a collision course with an angry wild buffalo with a pair of finely pointed horns in Pobitora.... led me to ride a cart full of decaying manure with an inebriated Captain in Morocco.... in short, it has given me the ability to look at life from a detached viewpoint normally practiced by people trying to attain nirvana.

I have tried to keep the nautical terminologies to a minimum but in the chronicles of a seaman.... ex or current, port, starboard, yardarm, gangway, mate are bound to crop up. If you can put up with these, as you have with Captain Haddock, come with me to a journey through the rain forests of South America, over the mighty Andes, through the terrible beauty of the Bering Straits or simply get chased by an Indian Bison in the forests of Terai.
