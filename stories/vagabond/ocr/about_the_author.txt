About the Author

Pralay Lahiry was born in the middle of
a catastrophe. Born in the year 1968 in
the sleepy town of Jalpaiguri in North
Bengal, he literally landed in water
since the most devastating flood in the
last century, hit the town. That bond
with water lasted a lifetime. He joined
Merchant Navy as a Radio Officer and
served for twenty years before deciding that there has been

enough water in his life to last two lifetimes and some greenery is
needed. One fine morning he put in his papers with the Captain
in one of the Shell LNG vessels where he was serving as an
Administration Officer and decided to pick up the camera and
lens and head into the forests amidst which he was born,
Jalpaiguri having been surrounded by forests when he was born.
and where he still finds peace and solitude. Instead of facing
typhoons and irate Captains he decided to change his profession

and capture charging elephants and rhinos.

This is a story penned, or rather, typed by him about his journeys
through the murky waters in British Guyana, his sitting in a
small hut in the jungles of Indonesia, travelling up a tree to avoid
the pointed horns of a wild buffalo in Pobitora Wildlife
Sanctuary and various other incidents which spans Guatemala,
Iran, Siberia, Nigeria, Alaska, Assam, Dooars, Mexico,

Philippines and a lot more.
