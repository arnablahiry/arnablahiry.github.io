The fight is on

He could trace his ancestry from the dinosaurs. The Pterodactyls
had ceased to fly. T-Rex did the pursue the Stegosaurus across
the ancient plains. His ancestors survived all the natural
calamities élan. However, as the centuries passed and the Homo
Sapiens started ruling the planet, their habit started to shrink
alarmingly. Finally, their home range shrunk to two continents
separated by oceans, mountains, deserts and forests. Huge tracts
of human habitations separated his ancestors and they survived
in Africa and Asia. In Asia they started beating a retreat but his
magnificent horn sounded his doom. The Chinese found his
horns irresistible as a means for expanding their exploding
population. Quack doctors prescribed the powdered horn as a
remedy for conjugal dysfunction and many other medical
conditions. Their prescription was not based on any medical
research but solely on the shape of the horn. The problem was
that, it could not be procured without the consent of its owners.
The owners flatly refused to part with their horns. The

solution...? Kill them and chop their horns off.

This went on for centuries till a handful of his ancestors were left,
struggling for existence in a corner of colonial India... Kaziranga
In the North eastern corner of India, amid vast grasslands on the
plains of the Brahmaputra River, flanked by the massive wall of

the Himalayas his ancestors fought a losing battle with humans

Just as it seemed that the battle was over and it looked likely that
the Chinese will have to carry on expanding their population
without the help of the horn of his ancestors, the then rulers led

Kaziranga, the fabled land of the One Horned Rhino was born in
the beginning of twentieth century. Slowly, over the years, his
ancestors made a comeback seldom seen in the short History of
conservation. From just about a hundred, by the time the century
turned, their numbers had gone up to more than two thousand.
However, unknown to him, the battle was yet to be won. Every
year men with big guns came in the dead of the night and
sometimes even by day and shot his kin and the precious horn
was still exported to the hungry markets of China at

astronomical prices,

His small brain did not register all these complexities. He was
happy with the green provided by the rich soil. He was happy to
survive the annual devastating Rood by the Brahmaputra. When
all else was submerged by the raging torrents, he was happy if he
could find a patch of dry high ground. He was happy even if he
had to share his grassland with the huge Wild Buffalos who too

were survivors. The herds of elephants, which too were under

The flood in the year 2012 was more severe than the rest. River
Brahmaputra vented his fury as only he could. Huge and ancient
trees got uprooted and were swept away in minutes. Animals
died in hordes. Yet he found a patch of high ground and survived

till the water receded. He was happy to have survived.

The sun had set in blazing colours drenching the lakes and
grassland and the forest in a burst of gold. The clouds had
disappeared over the horizon with the receding monsoon.
Though the ground was still soggy, the air held the promise of a
new beginning for life. The flood brought death but in death it
brought new life. Like the cycle of life, the flood gave back what it
took.

The grass was fresh and green and tasted sweet and crunchy. As
darkness descended, the Hog Deer sounded alarm calls. Perhaps

Towards dawn he felt like emptying his bowels. However, as per
instinct, genetically imprinted, he had to do this task at one place
only. He had already built up a big pile day by day not far from

where he was resting. This was where he headed.

The sky was faintly speckled with pink as the sun appeared on

Suddenly a piercing light broke from behind a nearby tree and
shone directly in his face. He felt a sense of doom. In desperation
he bobbed his massive head from side to side to avoid the light
when a rattling sound rang out from behind the light. The pain

His eyes were gradually fading and yet he could see the gleam on

with his massive horn but his muscles did not pay heed to what
his brain dictated. As he lost consciousness the machete came
down heavily time and again till his horn came apart and a
gaping, bleeding hole was left in its place. The men collected the
majestic horn ina filthy sack and departed as silently as they

came.

When sense returned to him, he wished that he was dead. The
pain was unbearable and made him lose consciousness again and

again. The damp earth no longer smelt of mud but that of blood.

This went on for hours but his life refused to leave him till some
men in uniform came with heavy guns. They took mercy on him
and he went down with a bullet in his heart, smelling the earth,
damp with his blood. Before he closed his eyes, he heard the
wind blowing over the waters of Sohola Beel carrying with it the

Kaziranga is not just a National Park or a forest or wildlife
sanctuary. It is an experience and a celebration of the success of
conservation which is under threat day in and day out. It is not
only the celebrated Rhino which is under threat but the less
celebrated but equally, if not more endangered species like the
Bengal Florican, Hog Badger, Pangolin, Eastern Swamp Deer and
others. If Kaziranga disappears someday, it will herald the
termination of the spirit of life. The battle is yet to be won. The
fight is on... in earnest.
