The road snaked up and up but it was a road.... a metalled road.
Subhash, Dev and myself trudged up but it was easy going, not
one of the Himalayan pathways which only the Paharis and
lizards were capable of climbing. I was wondering when the
difficult part would arrive as we walked past sleepy hamlets with
children playing and dogs barking. The road finally arrived and
lived up to my expectations. As I was admiring a lovely little
wooden house in a village, Subhash veered off the road and
clambered up a steep slope and looked down at me... “here

goes”, I said to myself and with a sigh, left the metalled road.

A few hundred feet up the slope and the path that we were
following all but vanished. The sun beat down mercilessly and we
climbed and climbed. Somewhere in the undergrowth a man
appeared from nowhere. Apparently, Subhash and Dev knew
him and they started a conversation. Since I did not understand
the dialect, I took the opportunity to lie down under a tree to

catch my breath and listen to them.

This man, though wobbling under the influence of something

guides of something while repeatedly pointing in a direction in

Old trees towered around and thrust their proud heads in the
cloudless sky. High mountains surrounded us on all sides. These
were not the snow-capped peaks but were high enough to shut
out the mighty Kanchenjunga range. There was no breeze and
despite being inside the Himalayas, the heat simmered even in
the month of March. Cicadas droned monotonously as we

walked on.
